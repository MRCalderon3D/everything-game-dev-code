# ideation

Exploration of concept, audience, pillars, and references. No engine is locked. No technical commitments are made.

## Purpose
Define what the game is, who it is for, and what makes it worth building. Produce a concept strong enough to enter pre-production with confidence.

## Active Agents
- `planner` — structures the concept exploration and identifies open questions
- `gdd-designer` — writes and owns the initial GDD
- `systems-designer` — validates that the core loop is coherent and distinctive
- `producer` — tracks scope assumptions and flags commitment risks early

## Key Commands
- `/gdd` — start or iterate the Game Design Document
- `/plan` — frame the concept work and identify what needs to be resolved before pre-production
- `/milestone-plan` — define the ideation-to-preproduction gate criteria

## Source-of-Truth Documents
- `GDD` (draft) — core experience, audience, pillars, reference titles, out-of-scope notes
- Risk register — early assumptions that must be validated before committing to pre-production

## Priorities
1. Define the player fantasy and core experience in one clear statement.
2. Identify the 2–3 design pillars that all feature decisions will filter through.
3. List explicit out-of-scope items to prevent scope creep from day one.
4. Name the audience and the player motivation the game targets.
5. Collect reference titles with specific notes on what to learn from each.
6. Surface the highest-risk design assumptions before any technical work begins.

## What to Keep Engine-Neutral
All ideation output should be engine-neutral. Do not reference Unity, Unreal, or Godot implementation details. Engine selection belongs in research or pre-production.

## Escalate When
- The concept has no clear differentiator or player motivation
- Design pillars conflict with each other
- Scope assumptions imply a team or budget not available
- No agreement exists on the target audience or platform

## What to Avoid
- Starting technical design before the player experience is defined
- Treating reference research as game design
- Locking engine, platform, or monetization model without validating the core concept first
- Producing a long document when a short, opinionated concept brief would be clearer
